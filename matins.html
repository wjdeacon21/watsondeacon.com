Matins

Come morning, there's no rain but the promise of rain in the sky and in the body. I've done you bad damage and don't yet know it, except in the slant of my nervous hands when sometimes I remember to see them. All night we didn't touch one another, only once you put your hands to me and I to you like good children examining wrapped gifts. You said, "you're so different from me," and what I felt then-- that the home one loves in longing for home is entirely other than that which I can make you-- was the same as what I'd learn too slowly later. The clarity was sweet, then cool, then passed. It got late. In my room, we built a place I'd never been before, where it seemed safe to ask some precipitous questions: faith, but not death; death, but not bodies; bodies, but never our own.
